## 2.1 Logo Safe Zone

The safe zone is the area around the SQLI logo that should be free of any visual information that could impact the legibility of the logotype. In other words, it makes sure that the SQLI logo is always clearly visible.

It's represented by the height of the stem of letter **i** in the logotype. Use it to define the surrounding margins for the material you're working with.

The unit of measurement is **"a"** = height of the stem of the letter "i" in the logotype.

```
         a
    +---------+
    |         |
 a  |  sqli   |  a
    |         |
    +---------+
         a
```

- Top margin: **a**
- Bottom margin: **a**
- Left margin: **a**
- Right margin: **a**

The same safe zone principle applies to the **Ascenders** element.

## 2.2 Logo with Partners

Please consider:

- The logos should be placed **next to each other with a divider line between**
- The **SQLI logo always comes first**
- Client logos vary in their format so they should be adjusted following the examples

Please reach out to your marketing or design teams for the correct usage of partnership logos.

## 2.6 Logo Placement

We prioritise placing the logo at the **top edge** in cases where the entire canvas is visible, such as on an A4 document or a social media post.

We also allow for the logo to be **free-standing** where necessary -- such as a profile picture or for wall-signage.

- **Top-left corner** -- logo at top edge, cropped slightly at top (preferred for documents, social posts)
- **Centered** -- logo centered in the canvas (acceptable for profile pictures, signage)

## 2.7 Ascenders Placement

The ascenders are essentially an abbreviated version of the main logo word mark. We use it in cases where the full logo either has been well established already or where the focus should be elsewhere and the logotype would potentially distract from the subject.

- **Correct**: Ascenders placed at or near the **top edge**, slightly off-center or aligned to a grid
- **Incorrect**: Ascenders floating in the **middle** of the canvas without context

## 2.8 Ascenders Usage

With some exceptions, the ascenders should **not be used completely on their own**; they must be accompanied either by **content** or alongside the **"Elevate. Digitally" promise**.

### Standalone Ascenders (with content)

The ascenders appear at the top of the canvas with body text below:

> We create digital experiences that elevate the performance of businesses everywhere.
>
> We Elevate. Digitally

### Ascenders and promise

The ascenders paired directly with the brand promise:

> [Ascenders] Elevate.
>             Digitally

## 2.9 Ascenders Misuse -- Don'ts

The following are **incorrect** uses of the ascenders:

1. **Don't use ascenders as a decorative wallpaper** -- e.g. a repeating pattern covering the full canvas
2. **Don't overlay ascenders on top of other content** such as text
3. **Don't use ascenders as a separator or divider element**
4. **Don't scale ascenders to the full height of the canvas**
5. **Don't embed ascenders within other words** -- e.g. "Hello" with the "ll" replaced by ascenders
6. **Don't enlarge the ascenders to oversized, rounded shapes**
7. **Don't overlay ascenders on photographs**
8. **Don't apply effects (blur, glow, gradients) to the ascenders**
